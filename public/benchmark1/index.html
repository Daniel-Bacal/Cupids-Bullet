Cupid's Bullet Game Design Document

By N.O. Games ™

Introduction

This document describes a game called Cupid’s Bullet which is a 2D dating simulator/bullet hell game. The game will feature many UI elements for the dating simulation part, and sprite-based animation, collision detection, and physics for the bullet hell part.

Technology

Cupid’s Bullet will be developed as a browser based game in the Phaser game engine, which is a popular javascript game engine developed by Photon Storm. WebGL/Canvas will be used for rendering. The DAW Reaper will be used for music and sound effects. Photoshop will be used for the artwork.

Backstory

You have been having a real struggle finding romance. It’s only 3 days until Valentine's Day and you’re trying to find a date for the big day. You’ve been hearing about this new dating app called Grÿnder (grin-dur) and decide to give it a go. However, what you don’t realize, is that this dating app has special features. You don’t just land a date, you have to fight for it.

Objective

Your objective is to get a date on Grÿnder for each day leading up to Valentine’s Day so that you can have a valentine. In order to secure these dates, you have to compete against other Grÿnder users. You must build the strengths of your profile so that you can overcome them to find your partner.

Gameplay

You are presented with a mock website that is the Grÿnder website.
  • Skill Tree:
    • Go down the tree for different archetypes, gives different attributes and abilities, ex. sexual orientation, different bullet effects, and special moves.
    • At the start of each day, you get a skill point to put into the skill tree.
    Skill Tree by Daniel Bacal

  • Stats:
    • Changes interactions with people in dating sim. Boosts multipliers during combat.
      • Intelligence - movement speed (boosts intelligent messages).
      • Humor - attack speed (boosts funny messages).
      • Sincerity - health (boosts sincere messages).
      • Flirtatiousness - weakens enemies (boosts flirty messages).
      • Jockness - damage (boosts jock messages).

  • Profile Creation:
    • Game begins with a login/sign-up screen.
    • Some special logins for cheat codes/easter eggs.
    • Asks for name, and asks 10 personality questions (each of the three provided responses affects your stats).
      • e.g. "When was the last time you read a book?", "Do you Play Sports?", etc.

  • Dating Simulator Core Gameplay:
    • Finite number of hand-crafted NPCs/characters w/ bios.
    • Chatting with characters:
      • All communication is through the chat menu.
      • Player selects an english text response from list of 5 choices (one response for each 'type').
      • Character responds with gibberish texts with emoji responses at the end, indicating how well the response did with the character. A corresponding friendship meter which goes up/down with the emojis. Each response has a type (e.g. funny, sincere, flirty, etc.) Each character has a preference for certain response types, and dislikes others.
      • Cooldown on how often you can send a message to a specific character (changes due to how previosu message was received).

  • Scoring Dates:
    • “Date” button you hit when you think you’re ready to ask on a date.
      • Difficulty of the pre-date battle affected by your relationship meter with the individual, and there is a rejection threshold where the person will say no to your date.
      • Virtually fight other people trying to date the same person. Must win the fight in order to secure the date. If you fail to secure one date any of the 3 days, you lose the game.

  • Ways to Boost Stats:
    • Go to the gym - Jock minigame (mash spacebar to lift weights: jock+).
    • Do math problems - Intelligence minigame (solve math problems: intelligence+).
    • Identify Haikus -Sincerity Minigame (identify the haiku from a selection of short “poems” from our random haiku generator : sincerity+).
    • Sending Messages - Boosts Flirtatiousness and improves relationship meter with people you message (Flirtatiousness + , Relationship Meter +).
    • Play along with a knock-knock joke - Comedic Minigame (type who's there? and *word* who? like you're listening to a knock-knock joke).

  • Days:
    • 3 5-Minute Days
    • 10 potential matches presented at the start of each day (Must accept and reject 5 each).
      • Can see their bios to get a sense of their personality type and preferences
    • Throughout each day you can message your matches from that day to try to get them to like you.
    • At the end of each day, you ask one of your five matches on a date
      • If you've built a good enough relationship with them, they will not reject you, and a battle sequence is initiated.
      • Only after successfully winning the battle do they accept your date. Not getting a date on any given day results in a game over.
    • At the end of the three days, you select one of your previous dates to ask to be your valentine.
      • This triggers the final boss fight which will determine whether or not you successfully secure a valentine.

  • Messages:
    • Send matches messages from each of the five categories (jock-ish, comedic, flirty, intelligent, sincere).
    • Responses are met positively, neutrally, or negatively depending on character's personality preferences.
    • If you have a high stat value and the person likes the stat, it will give a boost, and if they dislike the stat, it will boost the negative reaction as well.

  • Bullet Hell Core Gameplay:
    • Top-Down perspective.
    • A higher relationship meter with your date means fewer enemies to kill.
    • With each day, the battle has more enemies required to kill in order to win.
    • More enemies may be spawned than required to kill.
    • Player will have a health meter indicated in the GUI.
    • Player will fire on mouse click, and holding down triggers an auto-fire.
    • Pressing the spacebar will active the Player's special move (provided they have acquired one from the skill tree).
    • The player appears as and controls Cupid during the battle sequences, firing heart-shaped bullets at enemies trying to steal your potential date's love.

Controls

  • Dating Simulator:
    • Mouse cursor clicks to select options in dialogue, certain minigames, distributing skill/talent points, and changing tabs on Grÿnder.
    • Minigames:
      • Go to the gym - mash spacebar.
      • Do math problems - type in the answer to each math problem.
      • Identify Haikus - click one of two buttons identifying whether a poem is a haiku (is or isn't).
      • Play along with a knock-knock joke - type in responses to textbox (who's there? / *insert word* who?).

  • Bullet Hell:
    • W,A,S,D (Arrow Keys) to move left, up, right, and down respectively during the battle sequences.
    • Click to shoot, hold down for auto-fire.
    • Space bar for special move.

GUI

  • Screen Ratio - 16:9
  • Pixel Ratio - 480x270
  • Pause Menu - Gives you help and info about the game such as controls
  • Splash Screen:
    • Logo with the following options:
      • Log-In : Level select screen.
      • Sign-up : New Game.
      • View backstory.
      • Controls screen.
      • Overview of mechanics screen.
    • Level select screen.
      • Select from one the three days and the final boss fight.
  • Mock Dating Website:
    • “Swiping” pop-up panelGUI at the start of each day to pick half of the candidates presented to you.
    • Skill tree page at beginning of day (1 skill point per day).
    • Tabs on the website for various screens:
      • Your matches/chat conversations with a popup of their profile.
      • Your profile (stats/skill tree).
      • Mini-games - one tab for each minigame.
    • End of day screen - select who to date.
  • Pause button, but otherwise just a screen with a background image and sprites.
  • Game Over screen.
  • Victory screen.

Artwork

  • Logo for Grÿnder app.
  • Skill tree icons.
  • Dating Simulator:
    • Windows 95 vaporwave.
    • 30 profile pictures.
      • Assets such as hair, eyes, etc. and mix and match them.
      • Pixel cartoon style.
    • Backgrounds for images.
    • Symbols for each of the 5 stats (humor, flirt, etc.).
    • Skill tree.

  • Bullet Hell:
    • Simple sprites, simple backgrounds.
    • All sprites are squares.
    • Heart and circles for bullets.
    • Cupid sprite (idle, shooting, walking, damage, death animation).
    • Two enemy sprites - one male, one female (idle, shooting, walking, damage, death animation).
    • Animation for special moves (spacebar).

Sound Effects

  • UI Traversal
    • Message received notification sound.
    • Message sent notification sound.
    • Button click noise.
    • Spending skill tree point noise.
  • Bullet Hell
    • Player getting hit.
    • Player / Enemies firing bullets.
    • Enemy death noise.
    • Spacebar special ability activated.
  • Minigames
    • Grunt noise for gym.
    • Canned laughter for jokes.
    • Zen music/wind chimes for haiku.
    • Pencil writing noises for math.

Music

  • Five original pieces of music - one for each of the 3 levels, one for the end-of-day battles, and one for the final boss.
  • A simple tune/melody which plays during the minigames.
